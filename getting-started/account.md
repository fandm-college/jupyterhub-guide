# Your First Steps with F&M JupyterHub

## At a glance

- Request your account.
- Tell us about your course and software needs.
- *(Optional)* Set up off-campus access.
- Log in to F&M JupyterHub.

Before you can begin using F&M JupyterHub, you will need to request an account through Research Computing Services. Once your account has been created, you can log in using your Franklin & Marshall NetID and begin preparing your course.

## Request an account

Visit the Franklin & Marshall Service Portal:

[https://request.fandm.edu](https://request.fandm.edu)

Search for **Research Computing**, then select:

**HPC Cluster, Computer Science, or Research Computing Services Account Creation Request**

![Service Portal search results showing the Research Computing account request option]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/screenshots/service-portal-research-computing.png){: .figure }

<div class="figure-caption">
<strong>Figure 1.</strong> Search for <strong>Research Computing</strong> in the Franklin & Marshall Service Portal and select <strong>HPC Cluster, Computer Science, or Research Computing Services Account Creation Request</strong>.
</div>

When submitting your request, please include:

- The course or courses you will be teaching, such as **BIO227**.
- Any software packages you would like available in your course, such as **NumPy**, **SciPy**, or **Pandas**.
- Links to existing GitHub repositories or other resources you would like us to use when configuring your course, if applicable.

Providing this information helps Research Computing Services prepare your course as efficiently as possible.

## Off-campus access

If you plan to use F&M JupyterHub while off campus, you will first need to connect to the Franklin & Marshall VPN.

Instructions for installing and using the GlobalProtect VPN client are available through the Franklin & Marshall Service Portal.

{: .good-to-know }
> Research Computing Services recommends using **GitHub** to manage course materials, especially if you plan to update or reuse notebooks in future semesters. GitHub is recommended, but not required, to use F&M JupyterHub successfully.
