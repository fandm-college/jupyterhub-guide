# Continue Learning: Teaching with Jupyter Notebooks

## At a glance

- Explore teaching strategies for Jupyter notebooks.
- Learn notebook design best practices.
- Discover GitHub resources.
- Connect with the broader Jupyter community.

The purpose of this guide is to help you get started with **F&M JupyterHub**. If you would like to explore additional topics, the following resources are excellent places to continue your learning.

| If you want to... | Recommended resource | Why we recommend it |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Learn how to teach with notebooks | [**Teaching and Learning with Jupyter**](https://jupyter4edu.github.io/jupyter-edu-book/) | Our primary recommendation. Written specifically for educators and covers teaching strategies, classroom examples, assessment, and pedagogy. |
| See classroom examples | [**EPFL – Teaching and Learning with Jupyter Notebooks**](https://www.epfl.ch/education/educational-initiatives/cede/teaching-interactively/jupyter-notebooks-for-education/teaching-and-learning-with-jupyter-notebooks/) | Shows how faculty use Jupyter notebooks in real courses across a variety of disciplines. |
| Learn notebook fundamentals | [**Programming Historian – Jupyter Notebooks**](https://programminghistorian.org/en/lessons/jupyter-notebooks) | A gentle introduction to notebooks, especially approachable for faculty outside computer science. |
| Hear from Jupyter experts | [**Thomas Kluyver – Jupyter Notebooks for Teaching and Learning**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S7uUEwICDcc) | A presentation from one of Jupyter's original developers discussing educational uses of notebooks. |
| Explore community ideas | [**Jupyter Discourse – Teaching Resources**](https://discourse.jupyter.org/c/education/17) | Community discussions, classroom ideas, and practical advice from educators using Jupyter around the world. |
| Improve notebook design | **RCS Notebook Design Recommendations** *(Coming Soon)* | A concise guide with recommendations tailored specifically for Franklin & Marshall faculty. |

{: .good-to-know }
> If you read only one resource beyond this guide, we recommend **Teaching and Learning with Jupyter**. It was written specifically for educators and addresses many of the pedagogical questions faculty encounter when teaching with Jupyter notebooks.
